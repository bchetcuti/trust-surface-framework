TrustSurface Framework

A practitioner-led framework for identifying, measuring, and governing the observable trust signals organisations emit at their digital edge.

TrustSurface exists to turn digital trust into something you can inventory, evidence, govern, and improve — without relying on sales decks or internal assurances.

Read the framework One-page spec Worked example Library
Why trust surfaces matter

Most organisations govern internal controls. Stakeholders experience only what is visible at the surface: authentic email, legitimate domains, secure connections, predictable services, and governed third‑party dependencies.

  • Incident amplification: spoofing, brand impersonation, account takeover.
  • Assurance drag: repeated “prove it” cycles for audits, procurement, and cyber insurance.
  • Trust debt: posture decays through ownership fragmentation and configuration drift.

What TrustSurface is
  • Trust surface: the systems where trust is experienced (not declared).
  • Trust signals: observable indicators (evidence) describing posture.
  • Operating rhythm: Discover → Assess → Harden → Govern → Signal.
Trust is not declared. It is observed.
