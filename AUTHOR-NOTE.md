# Author Note

I built the Trust Surface Framework (TSF) to address a gap I keep seeing in organisations:

They invest heavily in cybersecurity and compliance, yet still emit weak or inconsistent **trust signals** at the digital edge — where stakeholders actually experience the organisation.

Boards and executives increasingly ask questions like:

- Can someone impersonate us tomorrow?
- Do we actually control our domains?
- Are our third-party platforms governed like critical dependencies?
- How trustworthy do our digital systems appear externally?

Most frameworks answer “Are we secure?”
TSF also asks: **“Do we appear trustworthy?”**

## Intent

This repository is published as **TSF v0.1 for consultation**.

It is not a certification scheme, a product pitch, or a replacement for existing security frameworks.
It is a governance lens that aims to:

- define the organisation’s **Trust Surface**
- measure **Trust Signals**
- improve trust posture through a simple lifecycle
- provide board-readable reporting and benchmarking via a maturity model
