# Digital Trust Maturity Model (DTMM)

The Digital Trust Maturity Model (DTMM) provides a structured way to assess how effectively an organisation manages the trust signals emitted by its digital systems.

DTMM complements traditional cybersecurity maturity assessments by focusing on **observable trust signals**.

## Maturity levels

| Level | Name | Summary |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Unaware | Low visibility of Trust Surface and signals |
| 2 | Reactive | Improvements occur after incidents or pressure |
| 3 | Managed | Signals are monitored and actively maintained |
| 4 | Integrated | Trust posture is embedded in governance processes |
| 5 | Trust Leader | Trust posture is strong, proactive, and transparently communicated |

## Domain-level maturity

Organisations may be at different levels across domains (e.g. strong domains but weak vendor governance).
DTMM supports prioritised improvement based on weakest trust signals.
