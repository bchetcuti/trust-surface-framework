# The Digital Trust Problem

Modern organisations depend on digital systems to communicate, deliver services, and interact with stakeholders.

Domains, email systems, cloud services, APIs, and software platforms now form the primary interface between organisations and the outside world.

When these systems function correctly, stakeholders rarely notice them.
However, when they fail or are misused, the consequences can quickly undermine trust.

Examples include:

- phishing emails impersonating an organisation
- compromised domains redirecting users to malicious sites
- outages preventing access to services
- vendor breaches exposing customer data

In each case, the impact is not only technical.
The impact is **loss of trust**.

## Trust is experienced at the digital edge

Many organisations manage digital risk internally through a combination of cybersecurity controls, governance frameworks, and compliance programs.

These practices focus primarily on **internal systems and controls**.

Stakeholders, however, experience organisations through a smaller and more visible set of signals, such as:

- whether an email appears authentic
- whether a website connection is secure
- whether a service behaves reliably
- whether third-party platforms behave appropriately

These signals form the **digital edge** where trust is reinforced or eroded.

## Existing frameworks do not address digital trust directly

Many frameworks help manage security and compliance, but they rarely answer a simpler question:

> How trustworthy do our digital systems appear to the outside world?

Traditional approaches focus on **controls** and **compliance**.
TSF focuses on **observable trust signals**.

As a result, organisations may maintain strong internal security programs while still emitting weak signals at their digital edge.

## Introducing TSF

TSF provides a structured approach to understanding and governing digital trust by:

- defining the organisation’s **Trust Surface**
- measuring **Trust Signals**
- applying a lifecycle: Discover → Assess → Harden → Govern → Signal
- enabling benchmarking via a maturity model
