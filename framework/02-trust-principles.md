# Trust Principles (TSF)

Principles guide interpretation and keep TSF from becoming a checklist.

## 1. Trust must be observable
Digital trust cannot rely on assurances alone. It must be supported by **observable signals**.

## 2. Trust failures occur at the surface
Most trust failures occur where systems interact with the outside world (email, domains, services, vendors).

## 3. Trust is an organisational responsibility
Digital trust spans technology, risk, communications, procurement, and leadership.

## 4. Trust must be continuously maintained
Digital trust is not a one-time achievement. Trust posture changes as systems and vendors change.

## 5. Trust should be communicated transparently
Transparency about trust posture (where appropriate) strengthens credibility and resilience.

Compressed form:

- Trust must be observable.
- Trust fails at the surface.
- Trust is organisational.
- Trust must be maintained.
- Trust should be transparent.
