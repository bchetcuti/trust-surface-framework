TrustSurface Framework

Purpose sentence: TrustSurface is a practitioner-led framework for identifying, measuring, and governing the observable trust signals an organisation emits at its digital edge.

What a trust surface is

A trust surface is the set of externally-facing systems where trust is experienced: domains and DNS, email, identity boundaries, public services, and third‑party dependencies. Trust is not declared at the surface — it is observed.

What trust signals are

Trust signals are observable indicators (evidence) that describe the posture of that surface: authentication and alignment, routing integrity, transport security, reliability, and governance artefacts.

The framework structure

The framework is intentionally small. It exists to make trust posture discussable across governance and delivery.

Trust surface domains

  • Identity boundary — how people and services prove who they are.
  • Domains & DNS — ownership, routing, integrity, and availability.
  • Email integrity — whether mail claiming your domain can be trusted.
  • Digital services — websites, portals, APIs, and the public application edge.
  • Infrastructure & platforms — the hosting and platform layer underpinning the edge.
  • Third‑party ecosystem — delegated trust through vendors and SaaS.

Operating rhythm

Discover → Assess → Harden → Govern → Signal is the minimal cycle that converts trust into evidence, work, and governance controls.

How to use it

  1. Start with the one-page specification for a printable overview.
  2. Use the worked example to see the method applied end-to-end.
  3. Use the library for the full document set (signals, maturity, board prompts).

What this is not

TrustSurface is not a product. It does not replace security frameworks or attack surface management. It provides a governance lens for the externally observable trust posture of digital systems.
