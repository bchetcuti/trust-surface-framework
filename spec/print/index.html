TrustSurface Framework

Digital trust, made governable through observable signals.
Status: Informative TSF-SPEC-1 Framework v1.0 Glossary v1.0 Last updated: 2026-03-06

Purpose

TrustSurface is a neutral, evidence-led framework for identifying, measuring, and governing the observable trust signals an organisation emits at its digital edge.

Definitions

Trust Surface: the collection of digital systems and observable signals through which stakeholders experience and judge trustworthiness.

Trust Signal: an observable indicator that describes the trust posture of a Trust Surface component.

Trust Posture: the current, evidence-based state implied by the strength, consistency, and coverage of signals.

Trust Signal Gap: intended posture ≠ observed evidence.

Operating rhythm

Discover → Assess → Harden → Govern → Signal → repeat

  • Discover: inventory, ownership, dependency map
  • Assess: scorecard, evidence links, observed posture
  • Harden: prioritised backlog and 30/60/90 plan
  • Govern: decision rights, cadence, change control
  • Signal: verifiable trust communications and published posture

Trust surface domains

  • Identity boundary
  • Domains & DNS
  • Email integrity
  • Digital services
  • Infrastructure & platforms

Positioning

TrustSurface is a lens, not a control framework. It complements ISO 27001, NIST CSF, COBIT, ASD Essential Eight, ISM, and PSPF by making externally observable trust posture discussable and evidence-based.
