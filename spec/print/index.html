TrustSurface Framework

Digital trust, made governable through observable signals.

Purpose

TrustSurface is a neutral, evidence-led framework for identifying, measuring, and governing the observable trust signals an organisation emits at its digital edge.

Definitions (normative)

Trust Surface: the collection of digital systems and observable signals through which stakeholders experience and judge trustworthiness.

Trust Signal: an observable indicator (evidence) that describes the trust posture of a Trust Surface component.

Trust Posture: the current, evidence-based state implied by the strength, consistency, and coverage of Trust Signals.

Trust Signal Gap: intended posture ≠ observed evidence.

Operating rhythm

Discover → Assess → Harden → Govern → Signal → (repeat)

  • Discover: inventory + ownership
  • Assess: scorecard + evidence links
  • Harden: prioritised backlog + 30/60/90 plan
  • Govern: decision rights + change control + cadence
  • Signal: verifiable trust communications

Trust Surface domains

  • Identity boundary
  • Domains & DNS
  • Email integrity
  • Digital services
  • Infrastructure & platforms
  • Third‑party ecosystem

Positioning

TrustSurface is a lens, not a control framework. It complements ISO 27001, NIST CSF, and COBIT by making externally observable trust posture discussable and evidence-based.
