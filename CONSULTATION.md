# Trust Surface Framework – Consultation

The Trust Surface Framework (TSF) is published as **v0.1 for consultation**.

The goal of this consultation is to refine the framework so it is practical, understandable, and useful for organisations seeking to govern digital trust.

This consultation is intentionally open and welcomes feedback from practitioners across governance, cybersecurity, technology leadership, and risk management.

---

# Why Consultation Matters

Digital trust is an evolving challenge.

Organisations increasingly rely on complex digital ecosystems including:

- domains and DNS infrastructure
- email platforms
- cloud services
- SaaS vendors
- digital services and APIs

These systems emit observable **trust signals** that influence how stakeholders perceive the organisation.

The Trust Surface Framework aims to provide a practical way to identify, measure, and govern these signals.

Consultation helps ensure the framework remains grounded in real-world operational experience.

---

# Areas Where Feedback Is Most Valuable

Feedback is particularly helpful in the following areas.

## 1. Board and Executive Usability

Does the framework help leadership understand digital trust risks?

Questions to consider:

- Can the concepts be explained clearly in a boardroom context?
- Are the outputs suitable for executive reporting?

---

## 2. Trust Signal Quality

Are the signals meaningful and observable?

Consider:

- Are important signals missing?
- Are any signals too technical or difficult to measure?

---

## 3. Lifecycle Practicality

Can organisations realistically apply the lifecycle model?

Questions include:

- Is the lifecycle simple enough to implement?
- Does it align with existing governance processes?

---

## 4. Language and Framing

The framework introduces several concepts including:

- Trust Surface
- Trust Signals
- Digital Trust Posture

Feedback on terminology and clarity is welcome.

---

# Out of Scope for This Version

To maintain clarity and focus, the following topics are intentionally out of scope for the current version of the framework:

- formal certification or accreditation schemes
- detailed vulnerability scanning methodologies
- compliance or audit checklists
- prescriptive technology implementation guidance

The framework is intended to remain **governance-focused and technology-neutral**.

---

# How to Provide Feedback

Feedback can be provided through the following channels.

### GitHub Issues (preferred)

Create an issue using one of these prefixes:

- `[Clarity]` – wording or structural improvements
- `[Signal]` – suggestions for trust signals
- `[Lifecycle]` – lifecycle improvements
- `[Governance]` – board or governance integration
- `[Example]` – practical case studies or use scenarios

---

### Direct Discussion

Constructive critique and discussion are welcome in professional forums and conversations.

The framework is intended to evolve through dialogue and shared experience.

# Guiding Principle

The framework is intentionally simple.

Where trade-offs arise, the priority will be:

**Clarity and practical usefulness over theoretical completeness.**
