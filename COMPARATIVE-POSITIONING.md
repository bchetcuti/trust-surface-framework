# Comparative Positioning — TrustSurface alongside ISO 27001, NIST CSF, COBIT

**Identifier:** TSF-COMP-1  
**Status:** Neutral positioning (non-promotional)  
**Version:** 1.0 (Public Draft)  
**Year:** 2026

---

TrustSurface is a **lens**.
It helps organisations identify, measure, and govern the **observable trust signals** emitted at the digital edge.

It does **not** compete with security or governance frameworks.
It complements them by translating “controls and programs” into **externally meaningful evidence**.

## What TrustSurface is (in one line)

> A framework for making digital trust posture observable, discussable, and governable through signals and evidence.

## What TrustSurface is not

- Not an information security management system (ISMS)
- Not a control catalogue
- Not an audit standard
- Not attack surface management

## Side-by-side (high level)

| Framework | Primary purpose | Primary unit of work | Typical outputs | Where TrustSurface fits |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **ISO/IEC 27001** | Establish and operate an ISMS | Controls, policies, ISMS processes | SoA, policies, audits, continual improvement | Adds a trust-signal view of what stakeholders can observe (e.g. email/domain posture, service transparency) |
| **NIST CSF** | Organise cyber risk management outcomes | Functions / categories (Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover) | Profiles, target state, controls mapping | Adds a “digital edge” lens that connects outcomes to observable trust signals and evidence refresh |
| **COBIT** | Govern and manage enterprise IT | Governance and management objectives | Process controls, accountability, metrics | Adds a focused posture lens for externally-facing systems, supporting executive reporting and decision rights |

## How to use TrustSurface with ISO 27001

Use TrustSurface to strengthen ISO 27001 in areas where stakeholders judge you externally.

- Treat Trust Surface domains as **ISMS-relevant asset groupings** at the digital edge.
- Use the Trust Signal Catalogue to define **evidence expectations** for key controls (especially email, domains, public services, vendor integrations).
- Include TrustSurface scorecards as an input to management review and continual improvement.

## How to use TrustSurface with NIST CSF

Use TrustSurface to make CSF outcomes visible at the surface.

- Map Trust Surface domains to CSF activities (especially Identify/Protect/Respond).
- Use signals to validate outcomes with evidence (e.g. spoof resistance, transport integrity, service reliability).
- Use the operating rhythm to create a lightweight cadence for reassessment.

## How to use TrustSurface with COBIT

Use TrustSurface to operationalise governance intent into evidence.

- Use TrustSurface to clarify **decision rights** and ownership for trust-critical systems.
- Add trust posture metrics to governance reporting as a complement to service and risk metrics.
- Use TrustSurface artefacts (inventory, scorecard, hardening plan, exception log) as governance inputs.

## The practical distinction

Traditional frameworks answer:

- *Are our controls defined and operating?*
- *Are we managing risk within an acceptable appetite?*

TrustSurface adds:

- *What signals are we emitting at the digital edge?*
- *Would an external stakeholder (or attacker) see weak trust posture?*
- *Do we have evidence, ownership, and cadence to keep signals strong?*

---

## Summary

TrustSurface is a governance-friendly model for **observable trust posture**.
It is most valuable when it is used alongside existing frameworks to reduce the gap between internal assurance and external trust.
